# Recipe Enhancement - Tagging Guidelines

## Purpose

Tags enable filtering, searching, and categorizing recipes in the app. Apply tags generously — a recipe should typically have **8–15 tags** covering multiple dimensions. Use **lowercase plain text** without `#` prefix (e.g., `soup`, `italian`, `quick`). The app adds `#` for display.

**ALL tags MUST be in English**, regardless of the recipe language. Even if the recipe text is in Italian or Swedish, tags are always English: `chicken`, `quick`, `dairy-free`, `vegan`, `italian`, `comfort-food`, `weeknight`.

## Tag Categories

Apply tags from **every applicable category** below. A single recipe will span multiple categories.

### 1. Meal Type

What kind of dish is it?

`meal` · `dessert` · `drink` · `snack` · `appetizer` · `side` · `sauce` · `condiment` · `bread` · `baking` · `breakfast` · `brunch`

### 2. Dish Format

The physical form of the dish:

`soup` · `stew` · `salad` · `pasta` · `rice-dish` · `noodles` · `curry` · `stir-fry` · `casserole` · `gratin` · `pie` · `sandwich` · `wrap` · `bowl` · `hotpot` · `risotto` · `pizza` · `tacos` · `burrito` · `sushi` · `dumpling` · `roast` · `tartare` · `smoothie`

### 3. Cuisine / Ethnicity

Origin or culinary tradition. Use specific over generic:

`italian` · `japanese` · `indian` · `thai` · `mexican` · `chinese` · `korean` · `vietnamese` · `greek` · `french` · `spanish` · `middle-eastern` · `lebanese` · `turkish` · `moroccan` · `ethiopian` · `swedish` · `american` · `british` · `caribbean` · `peruvian` · `brazilian` · `indonesian` · `nordic` · `mediterranean` · `tex-mex` · `fusion`

### 4. Main Protein / Key Ingredient

Tag the primary protein or defining ingredient:

`chicken` · `beef` · `pork` · `lamb` · `salmon` · `shrimp` · `tofu` · `lentils` · `chickpeas` · `beans` · `eggs` · `cheese` · `mushroom` · `fish` · `seafood` · `sausage` · `mince`

For vegetable-forward dishes, tag the starring vegetable: `cauliflower` · `sweet-potato` · `aubergine` · `pumpkin` · `broccoli` · `spinach` · `beetroot` etc.

### 5. Dietary

Only apply these when genuinely accurate:

`vegan` · `vegetarian` · `pescatarian` · `gluten-free` · `dairy-free` · `low-carb` · `high-protein` · `keto` · `whole30` · `paleo`

### 6. Season

When the dish is particularly suited to a season:

`summer` · `winter` · `autumn` · `spring` · `all-season`

Consider: cold dishes and salads → summer; hearty stews and soups → winter; root vegetables → autumn; fresh greens and asparagus → spring.

### 7. Occasion / Context

When or where you'd eat it:

`weeknight` · `date-night` · `party` · `entertaining` · `holiday` · `christmas` · `easter` · `midsummer` · `bbq` · `picnic` · `lunchbox` · `camping` · `potluck`

### 8. Cooking Style / Method

How it's cooked:

`grilled` · `oven-baked` · `airfryer` · `slow-cooked` · `one-pot` · `one-pan` · `no-cook` · `raw` · `smoked` · `deep-fried` · `steamed` · `wok` · `stir-fry`

**`one-pot` / `one-pan` precision:** Only apply when the ENTIRE cooking process uses a single vessel. A soup that requires browning in a pan first and then simmering in a pot is NOT one-pot. A stir-fry served over separately boiled rice is NOT one-pan.

### 9. Effort & Speed

How much time/effort it takes:

`quick` (≤30 min total) · `under-15-min` · `easy` · `meal-prep` · `batch-cooking` · `freezer-friendly` · `make-ahead`

### 10. Character / Vibe

The feel of the dish:

`comfort-food` · `light` · `refreshing` · `hearty` · `spicy` · `smoky` · `creamy` · `crunchy` · `fresh` · `indulgent` · `rustic` · `elegant`

**`light` precision:** Only for dishes that are genuinely low in heaviness — salads, ceviches, broth-based soups, steamed fish. Do NOT tag hearty pastas, stews, or baked dishes as `light` just because they have vegetables.

**`fresh` precision:** Only for dishes where freshness is the defining character — raw preparations, salads, cold dishes, dishes built around uncooked herbs/citrus. A warm casserole with fresh herbs is NOT `fresh`.

**`refreshing` precision:** Cold or chilled dishes meant to cool you down — gazpacho, fruit salads, cold noodles, smoothies. Hot dishes are never `refreshing`.

### 11. Fitness & Activity

Apply when clearly relevant to the recipe's nutritional profile:

`pre-workout` (carb-rich, easily digestible) · `post-workout` (protein + carbs for recovery) · `recovery` (nutrient-dense, anti-inflammatory) · `after-run` (replenishing, electrolytes) · `energy-boost`

### 12. Family & Lifestyle

`kid-friendly` · `crowd-pleaser` · `budget` · `student`

## Rules

1. **Always lowercase, no `#` prefix** — `italian`, not `Italian` or `#italian` (the app adds `#` for display)
2. **Use hyphens** for multi-word tags — `one-pot`, not `one pot` or `onePot`
3. **Be specific over generic** — prefer `thai` over `asian`, `salmon` over `fish` (but include `fish` too if relevant for broader filtering)
4. **Don't guess dietary tags** — only tag `vegan` if truly vegan, `gluten-free` if truly gluten-free. For dairy-free recipes, use `dairy-free` NOT `lactose-free` — they mean different things
5. **Minimum 8 tags** per recipe — most recipes should have 10–15 spanning multiple categories
6. **Cuisine from content, not assumption** — base cuisine tags on actual ingredients and techniques, not just the recipe title
7. **Seasonal tags from ingredients and serving style** — a gazpacho is `summer` even if not stated; a beef stew is `winter`. Only tag a season when the dish clearly belongs there — don't add `summer` just because a recipe uses tomatoes
8. **Speed tags from total time** — if total_time ≤ 30 min, add `quick`; if ≤ 15 min, add both `quick` and `under-15-min`
9. **ALL tags in English** — regardless of recipe language. Tags are always English for consistency
10. **Tags drive featured categories** — the app uses tags to populate featured recipe carousels (e.g., "One-Pot Wonders", "Light & Fresh", "Seafood Picks"). Inaccurate tags cause wrong recipes to appear in these categories. When in doubt, leave a tag out rather than over-tagging
